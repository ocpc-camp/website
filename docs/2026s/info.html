Camp information

What does the camp consist of?

We believe that in programming competitions, the most important factor in success is experience, and the camp schedule reflects that. On most camp days, you will participate in a 5-hour training contest with your team. After the contest, you have the opportunity to listen to the contest analysis and discussion, presented by the author or one of our coaches. In addition to training your competition skills, this is a great chance to meet with other top teams and make new friends!

Rules

Contests will mostly follow standard ICPC rules, in particular:

  • Each contest typically lasts 5 hours.
  • Ranking of teams is based on:
    • Number of problems solved;
    • In case of a tie, total time for all accepted problems;
    • Each rejected submission for an accepted problem adds 20 minutes penalty time;
    • If there is still a tie, the time of acceptance for the last problem;
  • It is possible to ask for clarifications about the problem in case of possible ambiguity or error. The answers will typically be restricted to "Yes", "No" or "No comments";
  • The scoreboard is public and updated during the contest, except for the last hour, during which it will be frozen.

We follow the "1pc" rule: at any given point in time, a team may use only one computer. "Use" means to meaningfully interact with it — reading the problem statements from a second computer is fine (it merely mimics having them on paper), as is leaving something running idle on a computer the team has stepped away from, provided it was started while that computer was the active one and is not touched again until the team returns to it. Onsite teams simply share one machine; online teams whose members are not in the same room cannot do that literally, so the practical equivalent is that only one member uses a computer at any given time — we trust you to keep to it.

Using the Internet during a contest is not forbidden. However, if your team is preparing for ICPC or a similar contest, it is heavily discouraged; we recommend using an ICPC-compliant Team Reference Document instead. Communicating with other people or large language models (e.g. talking to other people about the problems, asking questions on stackoverflow or similar forums, prompting ChatGPT) is forbidden.

Terms and conditions of the contest system apply.

Contest environment

Our contests will be hosted on eolymp. You can read how submissions are tested. Participants will receive credentials by email.

Camp materials and silence period

We will have a silence period that lasts for at least 6 months after the camp, during which camp materials will not be released to the public. Therefore, we ask participants not to discuss the problems in public during that period.

After the camp ends, we will give all participants access to camp materials for private use. Please do not publish them without consulting the authors or camp organizers.

Most of our contests are fresh and developed for this camp. A small number of contests may be based on previous contests that have not been released to the general public. If you have seen some problems of a contest before, you can't participate on that day (and your participation fee will be reduced accordingly). We will privately contact participants who might be affected.

Onsite rules

We encourage onsite teams to bring their own device. Computers provided by the host university may be available on request, but their number is limited.

Onsite teams will have access to a printer.

Visa & invitation letters

If you need a visa to attend, please register as early as possible and let us know. The host university can provide invitation letters for participants who require them.
